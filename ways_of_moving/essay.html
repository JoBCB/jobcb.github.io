(Di)fusion

It all started in the port cities of Buenos Aires and Montevideo — the hubs for the encounters of diverse cultures, where you could hear different accents and rhythms which together found a common language: the one of Tango.

Tango is said to be born from the fusion of elements from diverse cultures — afroargentinian, afrouruguayas, european — later released back into the world. It maybe compared to an estuary combining different ethnic and cultural influences originally flowing in the margins of river La Plata: “Buenos Aires era el último puerto del mundo: los artistas y los marinos llegaban a ella con toda la música recogida en los puertos del trayecto. Y la larga estadía de los barcos en cada puerto daba tiempo para el contacto y el intercambio con los habitantes.” [1] From the beginning, Tango has been embedded long into various stories, beliefs, fed by the imagination of all those who wanted to be part of it. [1]

”El tango creo un turbo pasado irreal.” [2] says Jorge Luis Borges, referring to the “blurry” history of Tango and its legendary, quasi mythological past.

In between the techniques and the poetics of Tango.

Tango is a space for communication: al bailar, se dialoga constantemente sin pronunciar palabra [3], from its origin is about establishing a space where we can encounter each other. As in any communication system, there is a language and a media. In this case both are corporeal. Tango is a codified language system — which follows often traditional social conventions — and its media are the movements that unfold from “el abrazo”.[4] A discourse rooted in the intentions of the bodies: hablar y escuchar corporal. Tango is also a compromise between an introspective action and a continuous communication between two people. And most importantly we find here a deeper understanding that communication is not merely verbal, and that movement and silence play leading roles in the construction of vocabularies.

“Nadie se baña dos veces en las mismas aguas de un río.”. [5]

Every dance is unrepeatable - the ephemeral character of Tango is emphasized by the improvisation attribute. Even though there is a very clear attitude to Tango[6] and an established setting of basic steps, there is no sequence of movements defined.
An interesting reflection on the improvised character of this dance is how much it creates a certain tension and yet a great communicative power between the dancers. As no previous script can be followed, the dance couple has to be extremely aware of each other's bodies and movements. There is a building up, a shared construction of a choreography, which rises from the inter-relation between the expression of a will to move and the actual movement. The improvised nature of Tango allows for a potential politics to come. It is not possible to speak of an ideal choreography or set of gestures, there is a constant re-formulation of such.

There is a great freedom in the creation of the movements (improvisation), either by suggesting the next move or by interpreting the move.[7] This bring to question: how can the unfilled gaps between steps, generate new possibilities? In between every movement there is a world of virtual possibilities. If Tango has challenged its technique (see Tango Scenario vs Tango Salon) it might as well challenge its pre conceptions in regards the to myriad of possibilities generated by two bodies. A move towards an open ended imaginarium, to overcome current male/ female [8],self and other, and other binarisms, and bring other urgent questions to play.

Tango from/for the future

Capacidad de intervención?
How to change the common aesthetics of Tango and bring a new discourse?
How can we rescue the freedom propeller of its improvisation qualities from the moment it was born?
How can Tango intervene in the relation between of sensing bodies?
How can gesture/ (signified) movement provides an alternate (and existing) vocabulary of the political?
How the silent words of Tango de-tranquilize current politics/ or the politics of the Port/ City/ Polis?


It is relevant to rethink the space of and for tango, a Tango as a space for all, a space for shared concern, in which “every body” is included, and works together in the empowerment of the collective body. To let Tango use its improvisation character to form new organic forms of thinking the time and space shared amongst people and technologies. To let its future bring new and radically thinking towards questioning current paradigms.

In castellano “pareja” (or the dance couple) means:

Parejos = Iguales….

“Unidad”. Uno mas uno en el Tango no es dos si no que es uno. - Syncronicity.

“El Tango nace siendo una danza de improvisación pura. Hombre y mujer se abrazan y procuran un devenir; buscan un tiempo y espacio futuro en función de lo lúdico, en función del juego. Es un estado igualitario, por eso cuando hablamos de hombre y mujer abrazados, hablamos de pareja. En castellano pareja significa parejos = iguales…. Es decir que hombre y mujer tienen un estado igualitario y juntos en una circunstancia de coloquio, de conversación, se proveen circunstancias dinámicas que van conformando lo que se reconoce desde afuera como Tango.
Cual era la habilidad? Era una destreza muscular? Era una habilidad muscular?- No. En realidad lo que se proveía el individuo para si, era la posibilidad de poder confrontar la dinámica en un estado de plena libertad . Esa libertad no estaba para el hombre sólo, estaba también para la mujer, en una mecánica de construcción creativa que le daba la posibilidad fantasiosa a los dos integrantes de la pareja. Es ilógico pensar que planteando las circunstancias dinámicas en plena libertad, esa libertad haya sido para uno solo de los integrantes de la pareja.”
